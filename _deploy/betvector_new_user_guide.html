What BetVector Does

BetVector runs three times every day, automatically, without you needing to do anything:

🌅
Morning — 06:00 UTC
Scrapes today's fixtures and bookmaker odds, runs the football model, and surfaces every match where the odds look mispriced. These are your value bets.
☀️
Midday — 13:00 UTC
Re-fetches odds (they shift throughout the day), recalculates edge on every pick, and updates the list.
🌙
Evening — 22:00 UTC
Scrapes final scores, settles all pending bets as won or lost, updates your P&L, and records performance metrics.

You don't trigger any of these. Your job each day is simple:

1
Open the dashboard after the morning run
2
Review today's picks
3
Decide which picks to place with your bookmaker
4
Log the bets you placed on the dashboard
5
Check results in the evening

Logging In

Go to the BetVector dashboard URL — the owner will share this with you. You will see a login screen asking for your email address and password. Both are set by the owner when they create your account.

Forgot your password? Contact the owner. They can reset it from the Admin page. You cannot reset it yourself at this stage.

After logging in you land on the Fixtures page. Your session stays active until you close the browser or click Logout in the sidebar.

Logging a bet

After you place a bet with your bookmaker, click "Log Bet" on the pick card to record it. The system creates a user-placed entry in your personal bet log, separate from the system pick already auto-logged. This dual tracking lets you compare the model's full recommendations against your actual betting decisions.

Important: Log the bet after you have actually placed it with a bookmaker. Do not log bets you are still thinking about — this creates inaccurate records.

📈 Performance Tracker

Shows how you are performing over time.

  • ROI — net return as a percentage of total staked
  • P&L Chart — cumulative profit/loss plotted over time; hover for exact values
  • Brier Score — model prediction accuracy (lower is better; 0.25 is random chance, below that is skill)
  • Bet breakdown — filter by market type, league, and date range
  • Two views: System Picks (every model recommendation) and Your Bets (only what you placed)

Check this page every evening after results are in.

🏟️ League Explorer

Performance broken down by league. Useful for seeing which leagues the model performs best in and where value bets are most frequent. If a league is consistently underperforming, consider raising the edge threshold for it in Settings.

🔬 Model Health

Monitors whether the underlying model is well-calibrated. You don't need to act on this page — it is informational. Check it weekly.

  • Calibration curve — a well-calibrated model hugs the diagonal line. Bowing above the diagonal = model is underestimating. Bowing below = overestimating.
  • Rolling Brier Score — if this trends upward over several weeks, the model may need retraining. The system handles this automatically.

💰 Bankroll Manager

Tracks your money.

  • Current Bankroll — how much you have now vs how you started
  • Drawdown Monitor — how far below your peak you currently are
  • Safety Alerts — the system warns you if: daily losses exceed 10% of bankroll, drawdown from peak exceeds 25%, or bankroll drops below 50% of starting amount
If any safety alert fires, stop placing new bets until you have reviewed the situation. These limits exist to protect you from chasing losses.

⚙️ Settings

Lets you change your personal preferences at any time — no owner involvement needed.

  • Switch staking method (flat / percentage / kelly)
  • Adjust your edge threshold (lower = more picks, higher = fewer but stronger picks)
  • Change your stake size or bankroll percentage
  • Toggle paper trading mode on/off
  • Danger Zone — reset your bankroll, clear your bet history, or both. These actions are permanent — use with care.

Understanding Odds & Edge

Decimal Odds

BetVector uses decimal odds (standard in UK/European betting).

OddsImplied chanceWhat you win on a £10 stake
2.0050%£10 profit (get £20 back)
3.0033%£20 profit (get £30 back)
1.5067%£5 profit (get £15 back)
3.5028.6%£25 profit (get £35 back)

Implied Probability

A bookmaker's odds imply a probability: 1 ÷ odds. Odds of 3.50 imply a 28.6% chance. Bookmakers add an overround (their margin), so their implied probabilities always sum to more than 100% across a market — that's their built-in edge over the punter.

Edge

Edge = Model Probability − Implied Probability

If the model says a home win has a 55% chance and the bookmaker's odds imply 45%, the edge is +10%. The bookmaker is systematically underpricing this outcome. Over a large number of bets with consistent positive edge, you should generate a positive return.

Edge does not guarantee winning any individual bet. A 60% probability outcome loses 40% of the time — that is completely normal. What matters is placing bets with positive edge, sizing them appropriately, and tracking results over 100+ bets before drawing conclusions.

Why You Will Lose Individual Bets

This is the most important mindset shift. BetVector is not a system that picks winners. It is a system that finds mispriced bets. Even a perfectly correct bet loses sometimes — that is the nature of probability. What you are trying to do is:

  • Only place bets when the model's edge is ≥ 5%
  • Never stake more than 5% of your bankroll on a single bet
  • Track results over at least 100 resolved bets before judging performance
  • Never chase losses — the model will find the next edge opportunity

Paper Trading Mode

When paper trading is enabled (in Settings), every bet you log is marked as simulated. Your bankroll tracking, P&L, and performance metrics all update as if real money was wagered — but nothing leaves your account.

Run in paper trading mode for your first 4–6 weeks, or until you have at least 50 resolved bets. This lets you:

  • Build confidence in the model before real money is at risk
  • Understand what variance actually feels like (losing streaks happen even with edge)
  • Fine-tune your edge threshold and staking method without real consequences

Turn paper trading off in Settings when you are ready to go live.

What the Model Does Not Know

The Poisson model is built on historical match data, expected goals, and bookmaker odds. It does not have access to:

Blind SpotWhat This Means in Practice
Breaking team newsA key player ruled out 2 hours before kickoff will not be reflected in morning picks
MotivationA team with nothing to play for in the final weeks may not perform as the model expects
WeatherHeavy rain significantly affects Over/Under markets; the model only partially captures this
Referee tendenciesCertain referees are card-heavy or lenient; the model has referee features but they are approximate
Fixture congestionA team playing their 4th game in 10 days may rotate heavily; the model flags congestion but does not know the starting XI

Your own knowledge of these factors should augment the model's picks, not replace them. The model gives you a statistically grounded starting point. The final decision is always yours.
